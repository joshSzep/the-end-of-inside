# TIMELINE.md

## Purpose

This document defines the **temporal structure** of the story.

It tracks:

- major historical events
- generational shifts
- sequence of narrative events

Time should feel:

- coherent
- grounded
- minimally emphasized in prose

---

# **Macro Timeline**

## ~30 Years Before Present — The Marcus Incident

### Event: Partial Record of Exposure, Dependency, and Withdrawal

Surviving accounts indicate that a radical **HumansRHumans** lineage, or the breach ecology around it, made Marcus usable inside a live vulnerability:

- Marcus, a working-class man in recovery far outside the lab or policy world, encounters borrowed empathic states that meet real hunger
- access arrives in repeating doses; whether that rhythm was fully engineered or opportunistically exploited remains contested
- dependency forms around relief, welcome, and borrowed safety
- access is later cut abruptly, or later remembered that way after a messier thinning; the sequence does not settle cleanly

Result:

- psychological collapse
- suicide

---

### Impact

This event becomes:

- a foundational trauma
- a warning about curated dependency
- a false lesson that cleaner coercion could prevent a repeat
- a story later retold more neatly than the people inside it ever were

Suzu Sato (Second Bell) is deeply shaped by this event.

---

## ~25–15 Years Before Present — Stabilization Era

### The House Normalizes

Connection becomes:

- more stable
- more widely accepted
- culturally integrated

Key developments:

- social norms around consent emerge
- people learn to regulate depth
- connection becomes part of everyday life

---

### Cultural Shift

A new generation is born into this world.

They do not remember:

- pre-connection life
- early instability
- resistance movements

To them:

> The House is normal.

---

## ~20 Years Before Present — Birth of Rei Nakamura

Rei is born into a fully connected world.

From the beginning:

- she experiences unbounded connection
- she does not develop strong internal boundaries

Her condition is:

- unnoticed at first
- later recognized as unusual
- never fully understood

---

## ~18–10 Years Before Present — Early Signs

As Rei grows:

- people feel unusually close to her
- relationships form quickly
- emotional blending is stronger than normal

Subtle effects:

- others feel deeply understood
- but occasionally destabilized

These signs are:

- dismissed
- misunderstood
- not formally addressed

---

## ~10–5 Years Before Present — Observation Phase

Suzu Sato becomes aware of Rei.

She observes:

- patterns of influence
- people around Rei opening too far and returning too late
- structural echoes of Marcus (not identical, but alarming)

Suzu does not act immediately.

She:

- gathers information
- monitors impact
- evaluates risk

---

## ~5–1 Years Before Present — Escalation

Rei’s effect becomes more pronounced:

- deeper connections form more quickly
- destabilization becomes more visible

Cases like Yui begin to emerge.

Suzu’s concern increases.

---

# **Narrative Timeline (Present Day)**

## Day 0 — Baseline World

Rei exists in:

- effortless connection
- full integration with the House

She experiences:

- no friction
- no separation
- no need to question her nature

---

## Day 1 — Rei Meets Yui

- immediate connection
- rapid emotional closeness
- early signs of imbalance (subtle)

This relationship becomes the primary lens of the story.

---

## Day 2 — Pattern Widens

- Yui remains present to Rei the next morning
- at the neighborhood center, most interactions still self-resolve
- Daichi's attention keeps returning to Rei without clear cause, though he remains functional
- Rei answers Mariko when she might have let the moment pass, then feels the sentence continue working
- Mariko repeats the exchange back altered, misses her son's calls, and fails to re-enter ordinary flow cleanly
- Fumiko becomes the first grounded witness to name the social disturbance as something people do not come back from all at once

This is the first sign that Yui is not the only consequence.

---

## Day 2 Evening — Before

- Fumiko and Sano recount Marcus in slightly incompatible shapes
- the broad model is legible, but the cause-and-effect chain does not settle cleanly
- one remembered detail does not fit the warning-story cleanly and remains unexplained
- `fed in drops` lingers with Rei after the conversation
- Yui's message rhythm arrives a little too immediately
- Rei prefers to treat the present as different from the older story

---

## Day 3-5 — Deepening Connection

Rei and Yui:

- spend increasing time in connection
- deepen emotional overlap

Effects:

- Yui becomes more open than usual
- identity boundaries begin to blur

Still not recognized as a problem.

---

## Day 6 — Early Instability

Yui begins to express:

- confusion
- emotional overflow
- uncertainty of ownership

Rei responds by:

- connecting more deeply

This worsens the situation.

---

## Day 7 — Isolation Event

**Trigger:**
Suzu determines intervention is necessary.

**Action:**
Rei is forcibly isolated.

---

### Immediate Sequence

- Rei is mid-connection (preferably with Yui)
- connection abruptly disappears
- silence replaces shared presence

---

### Subjective Duration

The isolation lasts:

- minutes to hours (externally)
- feels significantly longer to Rei

---

### Outcome

Rei experiences:

- panic
- disorientation
- first awareness of self as separate

---

## Day 7 (Later) — Reconnection

Connection returns suddenly.

Effects:

- overwhelming relief
- the world gathers again around Rei
- she waits for the cut to return and it does not

But:

- a residual sense of “self” remains
- continuity is no longer assumed
- Yui's text reaches in two motions: words first, person after

---

## Day 8–10 — Aftermath

Rei:

- tests ordinary exchanges for continuity
- experiences false alarms that pass cleanly without restoring trust
- begins to show slight delays, checks, and imperfect restraint in moments of closeness

Yui:

- is slightly out of sync before she is overtly unstable
- greeting, warmth, and later responses do not always land together
- remains available to Rei without fully completing the connection

---

## Day 11–14 — Escalation of Consequences

Yui’s condition worsens:

- work conversations and small tasks seem finished, then answer again
- she notices mistakes late and must re-check ordinary actions
- the disturbance enters public space around transit, purchases, and simple exchanges
- Rei's presence can make things hold together for a moment without resolving them
- after Rei lets go or goes quiet, the disturbance becomes more noticeable

Rei:

- begins to suspect her role
- tests whether closeness can steady Yui
- sees that even a small answer or touch can become part of the problem
- gets Yui home, then leaves her message unanswered

---

## Day 15 — Pattern

Rei:

- returns to ordinary center work and tries to stay smaller inside it
- finds that Daichi, Mariko, and smaller room-level slips still recur around her
- is named bluntly by Fumiko as part of the disturbance rather than protected from it
- resists the wording, but cannot return the day to separate incidents
- ends the day moving toward the upstairs office and the older history

---

## Day 16 — Second Bell Surfaces

Rei learns:

- isolation was intentional
- it was targeted
- it belonged to a continuing logic, not an isolated act

Suzu surfaces as the living interpreter of that logic before she settles as a fully knowable person.

---

## Day 17 — Marcus Through Fragments

Rei encounters:

- clipped records, older testimony, and morally interested retellings that do not harmonize
- Marcus as worker, recovering addict, and lonely human being rather than pure cautionary emblem
- no clean settlement of when dependency began, how deliberate cultivation was, or how abrupt the cutoff truly was

What sharpens:

- borrowed closeness can be made usable
- containment can learn from misuse without becoming ethical
- later accounts keep trying to turn Marcus into proof

---

## Day 18–20 — Attempted Restraint

Rei tries to:

- limit connection
- hold back

Result:

- ineffective
- unnatural
- causes further instability in Yui

---

## Day 21–23 — Emergence of Control

Rei begins to:

- recall isolation
- recognize internal boundaries
- experiment with holding separation

First successful partial control occurs.

---

## Day 24–27 — Practice Phase

Rei:

- refines her ability to modulate connection
- learns to maintain self while connecting

Progress is:

- uneven
- effortful
- fragile

---

## Day 28 — Reconnection with Yui

Rei approaches Yui again.

This time:

- slower
- intentional
- controlled

Outcome:

- Yui remains stable
- connection does not overwhelm

This is the first proof of change.

---

## Day 29 — Final Confrontation

Rei meets Suzu.

They discuss:

- the isolation event
- Marcus as warning, false lesson, and contested precedent
- the ethics of intervention

Rei:

- understands Suzu
- forgives her

But:

- does not impose that forgiveness

---

## Final Moment — Silence

Rei and Suzu remain:

- aware of each other
- aware of possible connection

Neither initiates.

They:

- stay
- do nothing
- allow separation to exist

The story ends.

---

# **Temporal Characteristics**

## Compression

The main narrative occurs over:

> ~3–4 weeks

This creates:

- intensity
- rapid transformation
- emotional pressure

---

## Contrast

Two key time scales:

- **30 years** → Marcus, contested dependency history, historical trauma
- **3–4 weeks** → Rei’s transformation

This contrast reinforces:

> how quickly change can occur  
> when conditions are right

---

# **Final Note**

Time should not be emphasized with:

- explicit timestamps
- rigid markers

Instead, convey time through:

- emotional progression
- character change
- consequence accumulation

If the reader feels:

- escalation
- compression
- inevitability

Then the timeline is working.
