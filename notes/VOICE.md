# VOICE.md

## Overview

The voice of *The End of Inside* should reflect a world where:

- understanding is immediate
- language is secondary
- silence carries meaning

The prose should be:

- clean
- restrained
- precise

Avoid:

- over-explaining
- heavy exposition
- decorative language

Favor:

- clarity
- emotional accuracy
- subtle shifts in perception

---

## Narrative Voice

### Style

- Close third-person, primarily following Rei
- Minimal filtering (“she felt,” “she thought” should be used sparingly)
- Sensory-emotional descriptions over intellectual analysis

The narration should feel:

- present
- grounded
- slightly detached at times

Especially early on, Rei’s perspective lacks strong boundaries.

This should be reflected in:

- blended perceptions
- ambiguous ownership of thoughts and feelings

---

### Evolution of Narrative Voice

**Act I — Fluid**
- thoughts and emotions blend freely
- unclear distinctions between Rei and others
- sentences may drift slightly without sharp edges

**Act II — Disrupted**
- sharper, more fragmented
- isolation introduces harsh internal clarity
- more explicit “I vs other” awareness begins to form

**Act III — Intentional**
- precise and controlled
- clear distinctions between self and other
- language becomes more deliberate

---

## Dialogue Philosophy

Dialogue is not the primary vehicle of communication.

Characters often:

- say less than they know
- rely on shared understanding
- leave things unfinished

Important:

> What is not said matters as much as what is said.

Silence, pauses, and withheld connection are meaningful.

# Character Voice Direction

## **Rei Nakamura**

### Core Traits

- open
- gentle
- perceptive
- lacks instinct for separation (early)

---

### Act I Voice

Rei speaks:

- simply
- without defensiveness
- without hesitation

She does not:

- assert herself strongly
- question emotional blending
- distinguish clearly between perspectives

Her dialogue often feels:

- inclusive
- soft
- slightly unanchored

Example tone:

> “It’s okay. I’m here.”  
> “I think… we’re the same right now.”

---

### Act II Voice

Rei becomes:

- uncertain
- more self-aware
- slightly destabilized

She begins to:

- question what belongs to her
- hesitate before connecting
- search for language to describe separation

Her speech includes:

- pauses
- corrections
- unfinished thoughts

Example tone:

> “Wait… was that me?”  
> “I don’t know where that started.”

---

### Act III Voice

Rei becomes:

- precise
- intentional
- calm, but not detached

She chooses:

- when to connect
- how deeply to connect
- what to withhold

Her voice is:

- measured
- respectful of boundaries
- quietly authoritative

Example tone:

> “I can feel you. But I’m staying here.”  
> “I understand. I’m not going to take it from you.”

---

## **Yui Takahashi**

### Core Traits

- warm
- curious
- emotionally receptive
- vulnerable to influence

---

### Early Voice

Yui speaks:

- openly
- with curiosity
- with emotional immediacy

She is comfortable expressing:

- confusion
- excitement
- closeness

Example tone:

> “That felt… really clear.”  
> “Is it always like this with you?”

---

### Mid Voice (Destabilization)

As Yui is affected by Rei:

- her speech becomes uncertain
- identity confusion appears
- emotional tone fluctuates

She may:

- contradict herself
- lose track of what she’s saying
- ask grounding questions

Example tone:

> “Wait, I thought—no, that wasn’t…”  
> “I don’t know if that’s mine.”

---

### Late Voice

After recovery begins:

- slower
- more cautious
- more aware of boundaries

She speaks with:

- care
- slight hesitation
- regained sense of self

Example tone:

> “Can we go slower?”  
> “I want to stay… me.”

---

## **Suzu Sato (Second Bell)**

### Core Traits

- controlled
- disciplined
- emotionally guarded
- deeply shaped by past trauma

---

### General Voice

Suzu speaks:

- directly
- without embellishment
- without emotional leakage

She avoids:

- vulnerability
- unnecessary explanation
- emotional merging

Her language is:

- precise
- restrained
- deliberate

Example tone:

> “You’re causing harm.”  
> “This was necessary.”

---

### Emotional Underlayer

Though controlled, Suzu is not empty.

Her restraint comes from:

- fear
- responsibility
- memory of Marcus

Occasionally:

- a slight crack appears
- a hesitation
- a shift in tone

But she quickly corrects it.

---

### Final Conversation Voice

In the final scene:

- she remains controlled
- but is more present

She does not soften dramatically.

But:

- she listens
- she does not interrupt
- she allows space

Example tone:

> “I didn’t expect you to understand.”  
> “That doesn’t change what I did.”

---

## **Secondary Characters**

### General Guidance

All other characters should:

- feel grounded and distinct
- reflect the norms of the connected world

They should not:

- over-explain connection
- react as if the world is unusual

---

### Key Distinction

The most important variation between characters is:

> how comfortable they are with connection

This should influence:

- pacing of speech
- willingness to share
- emotional exposure

---

## **Silence as Voice**

Silence is an active element.

It can represent:

- respect
- hesitation
- fear
- control

Especially in the final chapter:

> silence is the conclusion

Do not rush it.

Let it sit.

---

## **Final Note**

Every character’s voice should reflect their relationship to:

- connection
- boundaries
- selfhood

If two characters sound interchangeable,

the distinction is not strong enough.

Refine until:

- Rei feels open but evolving
- Yui feels affected and fragile
- Suzu feels controlled and intentional

The reader should be able to identify the speaker

without dialogue tags.
